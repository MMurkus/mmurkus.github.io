CSC-1210: Nature Blog

I chose to go with a nature blog because I've travelled to a few places like Arizona and the Virgin Islands over the past few years. These trips also included some of my personal stories that shows my perspective and how I grew from these events and what I took away from them. You can view the webpage by clicking here! GAM-2252: Rap Off

I worked with a team of three to create a card game for our second project in this class. The idea behind it is similar to how Cards Against Humanity is played. Players draw three cards and must make a rap out of them within two minutes, then all of the players not rapping must vote on who had the best rap in the round. I collaborated on coming up with some of the cards, but I mostly printed out the cards, physically put them together, and used Canva to write and design the rules of the game. I also suggested the idea of creating a cardbord box to hold everything that was required to play and integrating cards from Cards Against Humanity into the game in another sheet of rules.


GAM-2252: Stormsailers

For our first project in this course, we were instructed to create a board game within the span of four weeks. Working in a group of four, we created a prototype of Stormsailers, a game that can have 2-4 players play simutaneously. The game involves each player moving around the board and collecting the wooden pieces on the map, called treasure tokens. Once the player collects a certain amount, they can move to 1 of the 4 islands and collect a gem, which they must move back to the middle of the board (a.k.a. the eye of the storm) to win. Along the way, players can land on spaces labeled M or M2 to draw 1 or 2 mystery cards, respectively. These cards can either be play cards that can be used at any time or effect cards that happen immediately and usually rotate a ring on the board or place a black turbulence token on the board, which players can't pass through. I was responsible for coming up with the turbulence mechanic, creating cards like Boarding Party and Changing Course, and creating the rules for our game.
